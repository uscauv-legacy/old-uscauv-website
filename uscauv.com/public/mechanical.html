Mechanical Overview

The mechanical design philosophy for the SeaBee AUV is intended to create a modular, compact, and lightweight AUV. The single-hull design with an internal rack system enables easy removal and centralized access to electronics while the external frame provides support for long term development by allowing individual component redesigns with little to no effect on the overall structure of the AUV.

The AUV's structural design consists of a main hull surrounded by various components mounted on an octagonal frame. Improvements to the previous iteration include a redesigned hull, two external battery enclosures, a stand-alone sensor housing, and a sonar case. Underwater electrical connections are now made via neoprene wet-pluggable connectors.



Hull


The vehicle's hull is a cast acrylic, 8.5 inch diameter, cylindrical enclosure spanning a length of 12 inches. The acrylic is 1/8" thick, and was selected to reduce unnecessary weight, eliminate potential corrosion issues, and its transparency allows visual information from within the hull to be accessible. It is enclosed by an aluminum endcap on one side, and an aluminum collar on the other, held together by four tension rods. It is kept water tight by the use of two gaskets. The hull's collar attaches to an aluminum connector plate rigidly mounted to the vehicle's frame. The interface between the plate and collar is sealed by the use of two EPDM O-rings in a bore seal configuration.



External Frame


The external frame consists of an octagonal cage surrounding the main hull. Each octagon measures 10.836" around an inscribed circle. Panels constructed of T6061 anodized aluminum measuring 7" x 4.5" x 3/16" make up the walls of the octagonal cage resulting in a total of 24 panels. Each panel is associated with a certain location of the frame and a component. In addition, panels can move around the frame easily if design changes are necessary. The front and back surfaces of the octagonal cage serve as mounts for the depth thrusters and the flotation structure.

These panels are designed around a common panel template in SolidWorks and can be quickly machined through a laser-cutting process and then anodized. The panel redesigns dramatically reduce the design and manufacturing time of the Seabee AUV.



Liquid Cooling


The SeaBee AUV takes advantage of a unique liquid-cooling system consisting of custom aluminum cooling block, an external radiator and a circulation pump to cool the quad-core processor and other highly thermally demanding elements such as H-bridge motor drivers. The cooling block is mounted to the computer heat-spreader to cool the CPU while simultaneously drawing heat away from the H-bridges.







Battery Pods


Two aluminum battery enclosures are mounted to the underside of the vehicle's frame. This frees up space for the electrical systems inside the primary hull. Battery pods can be hot swapped, allowing for extended runtime. The battery enclosures feature a rectangular design in order to compactly fit the rectangular batteries. They are sealed via an O-ring in a face-seal conﬁguration. They are also fitted with pressure relief valves as a safety measure to prevent the buildup of hazardous pressure levels.
