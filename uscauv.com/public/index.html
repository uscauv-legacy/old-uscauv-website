University of Southern California Autonomous Underwater Vehicle

Who are we?

USC Autonomous Underwater Vehicle Team is a student-run organization that works to advance underwater robotics research and methods at USC. We are committed to researching, designing, and building a completely autonomous underwater vehicle (AUV). Our organization is broken up into software, electrical, and mechanical divisions that each develop cutting-edge technological methods for implementation on the team's vehicle. Through creation of an underwater vehicle, USC AUV teaches real-world engineering techniques and provides an insightful, knowledgeable experience to its members.

Since 2003, USC AUV has consistently participated in the annual International Autonomous Underwater Vehicle (AUV) competition of the Association for Unmanned Vehicle Systems International (AUVSI). The competition, hosted by AUVSI and the Office of Naval Research (ONR), challenges top universities from around the world to design, build and program autonomous underwater vehicles capable of completing a series of tasks. These tasks are designed to represent real applications of underwater robotics currently encountered in industry and academia.

Through creation of an AUV, the USC AUV Team aims to:

  • Develop new autonomous underwater robotic technologies and methods.
  • Provide a unique, rewarding experience for every member.
  • Compete with and stand out among top universities from across the world.
